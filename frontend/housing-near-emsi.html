EMSI (École Marocaine des Sciences de l'Ingénieur) has two main campuses — one in Casablanca (Maarif/Gauthier area) and one in Rabat (Agdal). Housing costs and neighborhood choices differ significantly between the two cities. This guide covers both, so you can plan before arrival.

🚌 Getting to EMSI

Casablanca

  • Tramway T1/T2 — Serves Maarif, Bourgone, Ain Sebaa and Ben M'sik. Frequent and affordable (~7 MAD per trip).
  • Bus RATC — Multiple lines serving the Maarif area from most neighborhoods.
  • Petit taxi / Careem — 20–40 MAD from most central neighborhoods. Quick for morning commutes.

Rabat

  • Tram L1 — Connects Hassan, Agdal, and Al Irfane. Direct to the EMSI Agdal area.
  • Bus RATC Rabat — Coverage across all main student neighborhoods.
  • Bike/scooter — Agdal is flat and bikeable for those living within 3 km of campus.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average rent near EMSI Casablanca?

Expect 2,200–5,000 MAD/month for a studio in Maarif or Bourgone. Shared rooms in Belvédère or Ain Sebaa run 1,500–2,800 MAD per person.

What is the average rent near EMSI Rabat?

Studios in Agdal cost 2,500–5,000 MAD. Sharing in Hassan or Al Irfane brings costs to 1,500–2,500 MAD per person — a much more manageable budget.

Which neighborhoods are best for EMSI Casablanca students?

Maarif and Gauthier are walking distance but expensive. Bourgone and Belvédère offer a good balance of price and commute time (10–15 min by tram).

Is sharing an apartment near EMSI worth it?

Yes — colocation is the standard among EMSI students. A 3-bedroom in Maarif shared 3-ways costs roughly 1,800–2,200 MAD per person, saving you 40–50% vs a solo studio.

How do I find a roommate near EMSI?

Use RoastMyDorm's roommate matching tool, or search EMSI's official student Facebook groups. The student union also maintains a notice board at the campus entrance.
